Keele Pitch Database - Psychophysics Tests

This directory contains a number of well known stimuli that generate pitch percepts. For more information see: Meddis and Hewitt, JASA 89(6) 1991.

All signals are coded as 16 bit signed integers and sampled at 20kHz. All files are stored in Intel file format. 

0: pure tones

These files contain pure tones, as shown in the file names: 2kHz, 1kHz 500 Hz 250Hz and 100Hz 

  1. sine2000.dat
  2. sine1000.dat
  3. sine500.dat
  4. sine250.dat
  5. sine100.dat

A: harmonic complexes with resolved and unresolved 

A1:  Missing Fundamental 2,3,4th harmonic of 200Hz = 600, 800 & 1000Hz (resolved harmonics) 

  1. rescplx.dat

A2: second harmonic complex: unresolved harmonics 14,15,16th of 200Hz (unresolved complex)

  1. unrecplx.dat

B: ambiguous pitch

generated after Schouten et al (1962) by 100% AM modulation of a 1990 carrier with a 199Hz envelope

  1. ambigpit.dat

C: pitch shift stimuli...

first six harmonics of 100Hz, shifted in steps of 25Hz  

  1. pitchsh1.dat        100 200 300 400 500 600 Hz
  2. pitchsh2.dat        125 225 325 425 525 625 Hz
  3. pitchsh3.dat        150 250 350 450 550 650 Hz
  4. pitchsh4.dat        175 275 375 475 575 675 Hz
  5. pitchsh5.dat        200 300 400 500 600 700 Hz

D: Musical Chords

C major triad, combine G4, C5 and E5 to hear C3.

  1.  chord.dat

E: Repetition Pitch

also known as comb-filtered noise or cos+/-, this sound is produced by adding a delayed copy of noise to itself

  1. cosplus.dat

# cosminus is same, but delayed copy is subtracted from original

  1. cosminus.dat

F: Amplitude modulated noise

 100, 200 and 500Hz AM white noise (100% modulation)

  1. am100.dat
  2. am200.dat
  3. am500.dat
